How the levels are estimated About readability formulas

These formulas estimate difficulty from surface features — sentence length, word length, and syllable counts — not concept load or background-knowledge demands. A passage of short words and short sentences about an abstract idea will still score “easy.” Reading eight formulas together gives a sensible range rather than false precision. Lexile is proprietary and cannot be computed from an open formula, so it is not included. Dale–Chall and Spache are omitted because they depend on word-familiarity lists. Syllable counts use a standard heuristic and are approximate.
